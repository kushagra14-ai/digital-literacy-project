### **Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud.**



#### Fake Refund Scam (Realistic Fictional Scenario).



###### *What the Crime Is?*



UPI/Online Payment Fraud, which is used by criminals to defraud their victims, entails criminals fooling their victims into permitting fraudulent transactions using the Unified Payments Interface of India, which uses real-time payments.



The process of it usually occurring (Step-by-Step)



1. The target posts a listing of an item on a classified listing site (e.g., OLX) which makes the target available to a fraudster.
2. The criminal makes the calls under the guise of being a buyer or some bank official and says that he is sending him an advance payment.
3. They post a payment request link (not a payment), and they make the victim believe that the link is a receive money link.
4. The victim inserts his UPI PIN to approve the payment - without realizing that he has authorized a debit on his account.
5. The funds are immediately deposited in a mule account and withdrawn.



###### *Who Is Usually Targeted:*



The most vulnerable are first-time users of the UPI, elderly people, and residents of rural areas with no experience and digital finance as well as those who sell goods on second-hand markets.



###### *Consequences:*



The victims lose money instantaneously, and in most cases, the money is not recoverable because UPI transactions cannot be reversed. It is followed by emotional distress and decrease of trust in digital banking. To the greater economy, this type of fraud delays the use of digital payments. Caught offenders are charged under Section 66D of the IT Act, 2000, and the corresponding sections of the IPC on cheating.
