#### &#x20;Stay Safe Online - Checklist on Prevention.

#### College students in India.



###### &#x20;Account \& Password Safety



* &#x20;The passwords of each account should be very strong and unique, i.e. with uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use passwords different on each platform.
* &#x20;Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) in all valuable accounts email and social media as well as banking applications.
* &#x20;Do not give out your OTP, password and PIN to anyone, including those who purport to be bank officials or the customer care team.



##### 

##### &#x20;Financial Safety (Mandatory) UPI Safety (Mandatory)



* &#x20;Note: To send money only use UPI PIN: you never need to enter this in order to receive payment. When a person requests that, it is a fraud.
* &#x20;One should always verify the UPI ID of recipient before making any confirmation. Check the registered name displayed twice, then press on Pay.





##### &#x20;Device \& App Safety



* &#x20;Install only the apps found in the official stores (Google Play / Apple App Store) not the APK files that are distributed via WhatsApp or links.
* &#x20;Make sure that you update your phone OS and applications on a regular basis to address security gaps.
* &#x20;It is recommended that you should not use public Wi-Fi to conduct banking, shopping or to access sensitive accounts.





##### &#x20;Online Behaviour \& Awareness

##### 

* &#x20;Never tap on any unsuspected links in the SMS, emails or WhatsApp messages, even when they look familiar.
* &#x20;Check application permissions on your phone regularly and remove access to applications that do not seem necessary to you (e.g. a calculator app that may request access to contacts).





##### &#x20;Reporting a Cybercrime

In case you are a victim or a witness of an act of cybercrime, report it:



&#x20;NCCP: cybercrime.gov.in.

&#x20;Helpline Number: 1930 (available 24x7)



Early reporting enhances more probability of freezing fraud transactions before money is drawn.
